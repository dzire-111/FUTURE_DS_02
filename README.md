# Customer Churn & Retention Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most critical metrics for subscription-based businesses such as SaaS platforms, telecom companies, and online services. Understanding why customers leave and what factors influence retention helps organizations improve customer experience and increase long-term revenue.
This project analyzes customer churn behavior using the Telco Customer Churn dataset. The goal is to identify churn patterns, understand which customer segments are most likely to leave, and provide actionable recommendations to improve customer retention.
The project includes data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and an interactive dashboard built using Power BI.



## Objectives

* Analyze customer churn patterns
* Identify customer segments with high churn risk
* Understand how contract type, payment methods, and services influence churn
* Provide data-driven recommendations to improve customer retention



## Dataset

Dataset Used: Telco Customer Churn Dataset
(Link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/blastchar/telco-customer-churn)
The dataset contains information about telecom customers including:

* Customer demographics
* Subscription details
* Internet service types
* Contract types
* Payment methods
* Monthly and total charges
* Customer tenure
* Churn status

Each record represents a unique customer and whether they continued using the service or churned.

## Key Insights

1. Month-to-Month Contracts Have the Highest Churn
Customers on month-to-month contracts are significantly more likely to leave compared to those with long-term contracts.

2. Fiber Optic Internet Users Show Higher Churn
Customers using fiber optic internet services demonstrate higher churn levels compared to DSL users.

3. Electronic Check Payments Have Higher Churn
Customers who pay through electronic check tend to churn more frequently than those using automatic or card-based payment methods.

4. Early Customer Tenure Has Higher Churn Risk
A large portion of churn occurs during the early months of a customer's subscription.

5. Long-Term Contracts Improve Customer Retention
Customers with one-year and two-year contracts have significantly lower churn rates.



## Business Recommendations

Encourage Long-Term Contracts
Offering discounts or incentives for yearly and multi-year subscriptions can significantly reduce churn.

Improve Customer Onboarding
Since churn is highest during early tenure, improving onboarding experiences can help customers see value faster.

Investigate Fiber Internet Service Experience
Higher churn among fiber optic users suggests potential service quality or pricing concerns that should be reviewed.

Promote Stable Payment Methods
Encouraging automated payments such as credit card or bank transfers may reduce churn related to payment friction.

Introduce Loyalty Programs
Rewarding long-term customers with loyalty benefits or exclusive offers can improve retention.



## Project Outcome

This project demonstrates how data analytics can be used to identify churn patterns and generate actionable insights. Businesses can leverage these insights to design retention strategies, improve customer satisfaction, and reduce revenue loss caused by customer churn.
